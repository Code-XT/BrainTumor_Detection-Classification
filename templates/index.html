Understanding Brain Tumors

Glioma

A tumor that arises from glial cells, often aggressive and among the most common primary brain tumors.

Meningioma

Typically benign tumors that develop from the membranes surrounding the brain and spinal cord.

Pituitary Tumor

Often benign tumors occurring near the pituitary gland, potentially affecting hormone levels.

No Tumor

When no abnormalities are detected in the MRI scan, indicating a healthy brain structure.
